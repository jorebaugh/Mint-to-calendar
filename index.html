How it works

Three things happen, none of which involve you typing.

Reads the email

You paste the “your schedule changed” email — the whole thing, forwarded headers and all. It hunts down the actual schedule and quietly ignores the opening paragraph where Mint lists, in loving detail, everything it just took away from you.

Does the boring part

Brief, the event, and debrief get fused into one calendar block — start of brief to end of debrief — named by the period, with the sim as the location. The exact thing you were about to do 28 times by hand, minus the sighing.

Never phones home

All of this runs inside this browser tab. No server, no account, no upload, no “we value your privacy” banner that legally means the opposite. Close the tab and it forgets you existed. Your roster's secrets die with it.

  1. 1
    Copy your schedule email. Open the “your schedule changed” email, select all, and copy it — the whole thing, headers and all, is fine.
  2. 2
    Paste it above and tap Make my calendar. Each brief‑to‑debrief becomes one event, named by its period with the sim as the location. Click any name or sim to fix it; the toggles up top control reserve, available, and off/block days.
  3. 3
    Send it to your calendar. On iPhone/iPad, tap Add to Apple Calendar and pick Calendar in the share sheet. On a Mac/PC the .ics downloads — open it. Or tap G ↗ on any row for Google.
Times in

iPhone / iPad: tap Add to Apple Calendar, then choose Calendar in the share sheet to add them all.   Mac / Windows: the .ics downloads — open it to add to Calendar or Outlook.   Google: tap G ↗ on a row, or import the .ics at Google Calendar → Settings → Import.
