How it works

Three things happen, none of which involve you typing.

Reads the email

You hand it the “your schedule changed” PDF. It finds the actual schedule and quietly ignores the opening paragraph where Mint lists, in loving detail, everything it just took away from you.

Does the boring part

Brief, the event, and debrief get fused into one calendar block — start of brief to end of debrief — named by the period, with the sim as the location. The exact thing you were about to do 28 times by hand, minus the sighing.

Never phones home

All of this runs inside this browser tab. No server, no account, no upload, no “we value your privacy” banner that legally means the opposite. Close the tab and it forgets you existed. Your roster's secrets die with it.

Not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to FedEx, Mint, or any employer — just one instructor trying to make a fellow instructor's day slightly less annoying. Free, no accounts, no tracking, no catch. It's a convenience, not a system of record, so eyeball every event against the official schedule before you bet a show time on it.
